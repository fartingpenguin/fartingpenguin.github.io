Dear Reading,
It’s been 8 years since the first day we met, I can’t even remember when I started to enjoy your presence. At first, you were very distant, something I only explored when I was forced to in school or by my parents. I struggled to remember what each letter sounded like and what each word meant. I watched my older sister read books and laugh, and I thought, ‘How could she find you so fun and easy?’ I tried and tried, reading the simplest books. However, I just could not find myself enjoying your overwhelming presence. As I grew older, and learned how to use a dictionary to learn the meaning of infinitely more words. I started to understand you more, I started to see why people enjoyed reading. I started to read more and more, and the more I read, the harder books I could read. The more I understood. The more I noticed you. Street signs. Posters. Food packets. And of course, books. But it hasn’t always been plain sailing. In school, when we have to analyse or annotate. I started to hate you. I wandered, lost, circumventing the world. I thought I could survive without you, but I saw you everywhere. There was no escape and it was torture. Until, one day, I started to realize I needed books. I needed essays. I needed Reading. I needed you.
Much love,
Andrew
